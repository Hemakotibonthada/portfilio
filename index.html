My day job is keeping production systems healthy at Tata Consultancy Services as a DevOps engineer — containerisation, pipelines, monitoring, on-call. After hours, I run Circuvent Technologies (also known as HT R&D Labs), where I architect, build, and deploy products end-to-end.

The pattern that defines me: I see a real-world problem — a hospital that can't predict cancer risk early, a village that can't talk to itself online, a homeowner who can't trust their smart switches — and I sit down and ship the whole stack. Backend in FastAPI or Node, frontend in React or Flutter, mobile via Capacitor or React Native, firmware in C++ on ESP32, AI on local Ollama or OpenAI, deployment on Firebase or Vercel, containerised with Docker, observable from day one.

I love hard, multidisciplinary problems: a cancer prediction platform with an ML ensemble, a local-first personal AI OS with 13 specialised agents, a self-hostable Jira/Keka alternative, an algorithmic trading framework with defensive execution layers, and a smart-home suite that talks to my own ESP32 nodes over MQTT. Each project is a chance to learn a new layer of the stack and to prove I can ship it.

"I'm not a developer who happens to do DevOps. I'm an engineer who treats every problem — infra, product, firmware, model — as one problem worth solving the whole way through."
